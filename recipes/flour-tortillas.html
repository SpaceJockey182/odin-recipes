Flour Tortillas

Description

Mexico produces wheat as well as corn. The most important wheat-growing area is the flat, irrigated land in Sonora and the northern part of Sinaloa. Tortillas of wheat flour are common all over the north of Mexico, but they really come into their own in Sonora. There are the sweet ones, thicker and shorter, more like pastry, usually called tortillas de manteca; and then the largest tortillas in Mexico, as thin as tissue paper and about 18 inches across.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound bread flour
  • 4 ounces softened vegetable shortening
  • 1 scant teaspoon salt
  • about 1 cup warm water

Steps

  1. Rub fat into flour on flat work surface
  2. Combine water and salt and add to flour mix
  3. Work dough into cohesive mass
  4. Divide dough into roughly 3 ounce balls, about 10, and rest for 1 hour
  5. Heat griddle over medium heat
  6. Roll out dough balls to desired size
  7. Cook on both sides, about 15 seconds each
